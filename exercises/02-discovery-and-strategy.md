# Block 2: Discovery & Strategy

**Time:** 40 minutes
**PM Skills used:** brainstorm-ideas, identify-assumptions, prioritize-assumptions, product-strategy, value-proposition

## Goal

Run a structured discovery cycle on your product and create a strategy canvas. The AI draws on multiple PM skills automatically based on what you ask for.

---

## Exercise 2A: Discovery Cycle (20 min)

This exercise uses skills for ideation, assumption mapping, prioritization, and experiment design.

Paste into Kiro chat:

```
Read my product context, then run a discovery cycle for my product.

I want you to:
1. Brainstorm improvement ideas from PM, Designer, and Engineer perspectives
2. Identify the riskiest assumptions (Value, Usability, Viability, Feasibility)
3. Prioritize assumptions using Impact × Risk
4. Design 2-3 lean experiments to test the top assumptions

Save the full output to deliverables/discovery-cycle.md
```

### What to look for

- Are the assumptions real? Cross out any that don't match your reality, and tell the AI
- Are the experiments feasible for your team? Ask the AI to adjust if not
- Iterate: "The assumption about X is wrong because... Try again with this context: ..."

---

## Exercise 2B: Product Strategy Canvas (20 min)

Choose ONE of these based on what's most useful to you right now:

### Option 1: Full Strategy Canvas (recommended for POs)

```
Using my product context and the discovery output from deliverables/discovery-cycle.md,
create a Product Strategy Canvas.

Cover these 9 sections:
1. Needs — user and customer needs
2. Stand-out Features — 3-5 differentiators
3. Business Goals — target outcomes
4. Market — target segments
5. Value Proposition — why choose us
6. Competitive Advantage — what's defensible
7. Revenue Model — how we make money
8. KPIs — how we measure success
9. Validated with — evidence vs. assumptions

Also create a SWOT analysis to stress-test the strategy.

Save to deliverables/strategy-canvas.md
```

### Option 2: Value Proposition Deep-Dive (good for SMs who want to understand the "why")

```
Using my product context, create a Value Proposition using the JTBD approach:

1. Who is the target user?
2. Why do they need this? (jobs to be done)
3. What do they use today? (alternatives)
4. How does our product help?
5. What's the "after" state?
6. Why is this better than alternatives?

Save to deliverables/value-proposition.md
```

---

## Reflect (2 min)

Look at what the AI generated. Ask yourself:
- Did it capture something I hadn't articulated before?
- Where is it wrong? (Tell it — the second draft is always better)
- Would I show this to my stakeholders?
