# Block 1: Setup & Context

**Time:** 30 minutes

## Goal

Get comfortable with Kiro and the PM skills. Create the product context that all later exercises build on.

## Step 1: Verify your setup (2 min)

Open the Kiro chat and type:

```
What PM skills do you have available?
```

You should see a list of skills across discovery, strategy, execution, and more.
If not, check [SETUP.md](../SETUP.md) step 4 or [TROUBLESHOOTING.md](../TROUBLESHOOTING.md).

## Step 2: Describe your product (15 min)

Open `context/my-product.md` and fill in every section with your real product.

Be specific. The AI uses this as its foundation for everything — vague input = generic output.

Tips:
- "B2B SaaS for project management" → too vague, AI will give you textbook answers
- "A project management tool for marketing agencies with 10-50 employees who juggle 20+ client projects simultaneously" → specific enough for useful output
- Include real numbers if you have them (users, revenue, NPS)
- Paste your top backlog items if you have them handy
- Don't leave sections as empty comments — write at least one sentence per section

Not sure what "good" looks like? Check `examples/product-vision-example.md` to see the kind of output the AI can produce when it has solid context.

## Step 3: Describe your team (8 min)

Open `context/my-team.md` and fill it in.

Include your ways of working, sprint length, current goals, and biggest challenges.

## Step 4: Icebreaker — let the AI roast your product (5 min)

Paste this into Kiro chat:

```
Read my product and team context. Then give me:
1. The 3 biggest blind spots in my product strategy (be brutally honest)
2. What a competitor would say about us behind closed doors
3. One thing we're probably overthinking

Keep it short and spicy.
```

If the output feels generic, your context needs more detail. Go back and add specifics.
